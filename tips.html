Tips and Tricks

Here are some general tips and tricks to get the best from your brew:

Wash Everything

This is more of a necessity than a tip but get a good steriliser for all your equipment and bottles. Everything that touches the brew should be sterilised.

Extra Equipment

It is good practice to keep a spare demijohn for racking and a few spare sterilised bottles for emergencies or miscalculations.

Bottling Wands

A bottling wand can be attached to a brew bucket or the end of a syphon, and will not allow liquid to flow unless it is pressed down.

Corking

You can use a standard corker or you can buy reusable wine corks like the ones here to cut back on costs.

Sugars

When adding sugar, it is generally recommended to use dextrose, however any sugar can be used. White sugar will ferment dry, demerara will leave a residual sweetness, and honey can be used to create a melomel. You can see a list of fermentables here.

Carbonation

You can carbonate any wine by adding some priming sugar into the bottles when bottling. Variety of sugar won't really matter, but be very careful of the bottle and do not attempt to carbonate in a normal wine bottle as these are not designed to withstand pressure and could possibly explode! There's a handy calculator here.

Measuring Alcohol

For this, you'll need a hydrometer, and you can pick up a good one with a trial jar here. Now in order to accurately measure the alcohol content, you'll need to take two measurements. The first is called initial gravity, or IG, and is taken when the brew is started. When fermentation is completed, a second measurement is taken, called final gravity, or FG.

  1. Take a sample before fermentation begins. This is the IG, jot it down somewhere.
  2. When your wine stops bubbling, measure again and take this down too. Try measure a few times over a 5 day period. If it hasn't gone down in that period, this is your FG.
  3. There is a mathemathical formula to work this out, but it's much easier to put these numbers into a calculator like the one here.
  4. The result is your alcohol content!
